# Six Thinking Hats Protocol - Full Reference

## Overview

Six Thinking Hats is a parallel thinking method that separates perspectives into distinct modes, preventing conflict and ensuring comprehensive analysis.

## Execution Sequence

### Standard Sequence (Deep Mode)
1. **Blue Hat** - Define the question and process
2. **White Hat** - Gather facts and data
3. **Red Hat** - Surface intuition and emotions
4. **Yellow Hat** - Explore benefits and opportunities
5. **Black Hat** - Identify risks and cautions
6. **Green Hat** - Generate creative alternatives
7. **Blue Hat** - Synthesize and decide

### Lite Mode Sequence (Quick Track)
1. **White Hat** - Facts only
2. **Black Hat** - Risks only
3. **Yellow + Green** - Opportunities + Creativity combined

## Quality Standards

**Full Six Hats Mode Requirements:**
- All 6 hats represented
- 2-3 paragraphs per specialist
- Specific risks + mitigations identified
- Creative alternatives explored
- Clear decision: GO/CONDITIONAL GO/NO GO
- 500-800 words total output

**Lite Mode Requirements:**
- 3 perspectives (White, Black, Yellow+Green)
- 2-3 bullets per perspective
- Clear 2-3 line recommendation
- 200-300 words total output
