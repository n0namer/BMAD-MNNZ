## 6. Improvement Strategies

### If CLV is Too Low

**Increase ARPU**:
- Higher pricing tiers
- Upsell/cross-sell opportunities
- Usage-based pricing components
- Annual contracts (12 months upfront)

**Reduce Churn**:
- Better onboarding
- Customer success programs
- Product improvements (retention features)
- Lock-in mechanisms (integrations, data)

**Improve Gross Margin**:
- Reduce COGS (hosting, support, payment fees)
- Automate customer support
- Optimize infrastructure costs

### If CAC is Too High

**Optimize Paid Channels**:
- Better ad targeting
- A/B test ad creatives
- Improve landing page conversion
- Reduce cost-per-click (CPC)

**Increase Organic Channels**:
- SEO and content marketing
- Referral programs
- Product-led growth (viral loops)
- Community building

**Improve Conversion Rates**:
- Better sales process
- Self-serve onboarding
- Free trial optimization
- Reduce sales cycle length

### If Payback Period is Too Long

**Shorten via Pricing**:
- Annual upfront contracts
- Setup fees
- Higher initial pricing

**Shorten via Growth Efficiency**:
- Reduce CAC (see above)
- Increase ARPU (see above)

## 7. Stage-Specific Guidelines

### Pre-MVP / Idea Stage

**Estimate** unit economics using:
- Comparable companies (public data, similar products)
- Industry benchmarks
- Analyst reports
- Conservative assumptions (worse-case scenarios)

**Example Estimation**:
```
Comparable SaaS: Slack, Notion, etc.
Assumed ARPU: $10-15/user/month
Assumed Churn: 5-10% monthly
Assumed CAC: $50-150 (mostly organic initially)

Estimated CLV: $100-300
Estimated CAC: $50-150
Estimated Ratio: 2-6x

Conclusion: Plausible economics, worth testing
```

### MVP / Early Traction

**Measure** actual metrics from first 50-100 customers:
- Real ARPU from pricing experiments
- Observed churn (even if sample is small)
- Actual CAC from early channels

**Adjust** frequently as data improves (monthly recalculation)

### Growth Stage

**Optimize** unit economics at scale:
- Segment by channel (organic vs paid)
- Segment by customer type (SMB vs Enterprise)
- Track cohort-based retention
- Optimize payback period to <12 months
